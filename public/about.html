About SKUT

SKUT was founded in Ottawa by international student Russell Young, who arrived in Canada with a dream of building something meaningful—not just for himself, but for the city he now called home. As a student navigating Ottawa’s public transportation system, Russell quickly realized how expensive, time-consuming, and limiting traditional options could be—especially for young people, students, low-income residents, and newcomers without access to a car.

What began as a daily frustration—waiting 25 minutes for a crowded bus—sparked a bigger idea: What if getting across the city was as easy as riding your own scooter or e-bike—without owning one? That idea became SKUT, an Ottawa-first micromobility company designed to offer affordable, electric-powered personal transport for anyone needing to travel short distances quickly, safely, and sustainably.

SKUT is not just a business—it’s a mission-driven solution born out of necessity and backed by deep local ambition.

Our Values

Community First Design

We build our services around the needs of Ottawa, ensuring SKUT is a truly local solution. Our goal is to enhance urban mobility in a way that benefits everyone in the city.

Fair Pricing And Equity Access

We believe everyone deserves access to affordable transportation options. SKUT strives to keep costs low and remove barriers, making micromobility accessible for all residents.

Transparent Safety Standards

Your safety is our top priority, and we’re committed to clear communication about our safety measures. We continuously work to implement and share robust standards for a secure riding experience.

Open Collaboration With Public Services

Working closely with Ottawa’s public services helps us integrate seamlessly into the city’s infrastructure. We believe in partnership to create a more efficient and connected transportation network.

Zero-Emissions Commitment In All Operations

We’re dedicated to a cleaner, greener Ottawa. Every SKUT ride helps reduce carbon emissions, contributing to a healthier environment for our community.
